Aspirin

Description

A drug that reduces pain, fever, inflammation, and blood clotting. Aspirin belongs to the family of drugs called nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory agents.

Directions for Use

Aspirin usually comes as 300mg tablets. The usual dose is 1 or 2 tablets, taken every 4 to 6 hours.

Precautions

ITake this medicine only as directed by your doctor. Do not take more of it, do not take it more often, and do not take it for a longer time than your doctor ordered. Take the capsule with a full glass of water at the same time each day. Swallow the extended-release capsule whole.
